Nurture Merit &
Free Online Coaching

Identifying bright children from government school backgrounds and giving them a real path forward. Completely free.

The Programme

Talent is equally distributed. Opportunity is not.

Students from disadvantaged backgrounds continue to have limited representation in national-level competitive examinations such as CUET, CLAT, NDA, JEE, and NEET. Many capable students lack the awareness, guidance, and academic support needed to prepare effectively.

To address this gap, SATHI provides academically bright high school students with free online coaching in Mathematics and Science, with a focus on conceptual understanding, along with guidance and mentorship. Over the past five years, the programme has supported more than 200 students.

Programme Details

  • The programme is completely free of cost to students and their families
  • Classes are conducted for students in 9th and 10th standards
  • Classes are online so students in small towns and villages can benefit
  • Conducted in the evening after regular school hours
  • Follows NCERT syllabus for national-level competitive preparation
  • Volunteer teachers from IIMs, IITs, and top institutions
  • Activities build awareness of higher education, scholarships, and entrance exams

Eligibility Criteria

  • Written the NMMS exam and scored 100 or more marks in total
  • Have at least 2 hours every day to dedicate to classes
  • Can solve textbook questions and want to attempt more difficult ones
  • Want to develop deeper understanding of concepts in Science and Maths
  • Want to attempt competitive exams, scholarships, and talent searches

Where Our Students Are Today

Cohort 2020
Ningamma
Chikkabidarakallu Govt. School, Bangalore
NITK Surathkal — B.Tech Electronics

Ningamma's parents are farmers from North Karnataka who migrated to Bangalore. Her father drives a goods van and her mother works in a garments factory. She scored 619 out of 625 (99.33%) in her Class 10 board exam, securing 6th rank in Karnataka, and won free admission at Deeksha Integrated PU College. In March 2024, she secured 97% in her PU exam and was admitted to NITK Surathkal for B.Tech in Electronics and Communication.

Cohort 2021
Mahadev
Mudagunda Village, Chamarajanagar
IIT Kharagpur — BSc Physics

Mahadev's father works in a ceramics factory earning around ₹7,000 per month. He scored 98% in Class 10 and won state-level quiz competitions. He passed all 3 stages of the Rashtrotthana Parishat exam and was selected for their Tapas programme. He cleared JEE and is now studying BSc Physics at IIT Kharagpur, aspiring to become an academic professor.

Two Scholarships
Chandrika
Jagadal Village, Bagalkot
BMSIT Bangalore — Computer Science

Chandrika is from a farming family and studied in Kannada medium through Class 10, scoring 98.7%. She received free admission at Avanti Fellows, Hubli. Despite adjusting to English medium, the in-depth training from the programme helped her succeed. She secured admission at BMSIT Bangalore in CSE and also secured two scholarships, pursuing her education without financial burden on her parents.

NEET Rank 18,000
Monisha
Bannur, T. Narasipura, Mysore
MBBS — Hassan Institute of Medical Sciences

Monisha travelled 13 km each way to school by bus but took academics very seriously, scoring 98% in her Class 10 boards. She passed the SAADHANA exam and received free admission (including hostel) at SAADHANA, Bengaluru, for NEET coaching. She secured a NEET rank of 18,000 (97th percentile) and is currently pursuing MBBS at Hassan Institute of Medical Sciences, a government medical college.

Cohort 2023-24
Jyothi
Koila Taluk, Dakshina Kannada
SADHANA Programme — NEET Aspirant

Jyothi's family moved from Gulbarga to Dakshina Kannada in search of work. Both parents are labourers. She joined the online classes in 2023-24 and, though she initially faced difficulty with technical English from a Kannada-medium background, grew to participate confidently. She passed all 3 stages of the Rashtrotthana Parishat exam and secured a place in the SADHANA programme. Jyothi is now in 11th standard with her eyes set on NEET to become a doctor.
