Street-Connected
Children

Pimpri-Chinchwad, Pune. Working with children facing violence, exploitation and substance use. Supported by Educo India.

The Project

Protecting Children from Violence & Exploitation

SATHI's project in the Pimpri-Chinchwad area of Pune works with children and adolescents living in street-connected and high-risk communities. Many children face difficult living conditions, disrupted education, substance use, child labour, abuse, trafficking, and limited access to healthcare and protection services.

The project focuses on identifying children at risk through community outreach and mapping, supporting them through Learning Centres, counselling, education linkages, referrals, and child protection interventions. It also works closely with families, schools, local authorities, Child Welfare Committees, police, and community stakeholders.

Through awareness campaigns, school and community engagement, case management, and rehabilitation support, the project helps children return to education, care, dignity, and safer life pathways.

The project is supported by Educo India.

Our Approach

The project follows a community-based child protection approach that combines prevention, early identification, intervention, and rehabilitation. By working closely with children, families, schools, and local systems, the project aims to create safer environments where children can access education, protection, care, and opportunities for a better future.

Key Areas of Focus

  • Child protection and safety
  • Prevention of violence and exploitation
  • Education access and retention
  • Emotional wellbeing and counselling
  • Substance use awareness and prevention
  • Family strengthening and community engagement
  • Referral and rehabilitation support

What We Do

  • Community outreach and identification of children at risk
  • Learning Centres for education support and learning continuity
  • Counselling and emotional wellbeing support
  • School enrolment and education linkages
  • Child protection and rehabilitation interventions
  • Referrals for healthcare, legal aid, and specialised services
  • Family counselling and community engagement
  • Awareness sessions on child rights, safety, health, and wellbeing
  • Coordination with Child Welfare Committees, police, schools, and local authorities
  • Strengthening Sakhi Savitri Committees in Government Schools
  • Organise Deaddiction Camps (SATHI Camp Model) at regular intervals

Stories from the Community

Back to School
Aryan
12 years · Pimpri-Chinchwad

Aryan had been out of school since 2020 due to severe financial difficulties and a missing Transfer Certificate. Through the project's intervention, he received counselling, documentation support, and school re-enrolment assistance.

Today, Aryan attends school regularly and participates actively at the learning centre. He dreams of continuing his education and hopes no child is forced to leave school because of poverty.

Towards Stability
Nikhil Vakte
13 years · Gandhi Nagar, Pune

Nikhil had dropped out after class 7 and developed substance-use habits on the streets. Through continuous counselling and case management, the project team worked with the Child Welfare Committee for his rehabilitation.

Referred to a Child Care Institution, Nikhil is now attending school regularly, has overcome substance use, and shows visible improvement in behaviour and confidence.

Between Change & Challenge
Karan Madalkar
12 years · Gandhi Nagar slum, Pune

After losing his father eight years ago, Karan grew up with little supervision and developed substance-use habits. SATHI's street educators enrolled him in a de-addiction camp where he learned about health risks and participated in structured activities.

Though peer pressure remains a challenge, Karan has begun expressing aspirations — he wants to become a cricketer. SATHI continues working with him through counselling, follow-up, and CWC coordination.
