About SATHI

A national child protection organisation, working since 1992.

Who We Are

Three decades of protecting children

SATHI (Society for Assistance to Children in Difficult Situation) is a national child protection organisation working with children in difficult circumstances, with a special focus on children found at railway stations, children in street situations, children from migrant communities, and children at risk of losing access to education, care, and protection.

Founded in 1992 by the late Pramod Kulkarni and formally registered in 1997, SATHI began its journey in Raichur, Karnataka. Through this work, an important insight emerged: many children living on railway platforms were not abandoned — they had left home due to conflict, fear, poverty, or impulse, while their families were desperately searching for them.

Over the past three decades, SATHI has worked in more than 40 locations across 14 states and has helped over one lakh children reconnect with their families, education, protection systems, and opportunities for a better future.

At the heart of SATHI's work is a simple belief: every child deserves safety, dignity, care, and the opportunity to grow in a nurturing environment.

Vision

To ensure that every child lives in a safe, secure, and nurturing environment, with the belief that family is the best place for a child to grow with self-confidence, self-worth, and dignity.

Mission
  • Create safe and protective environments for children in vulnerable circumstances
  • Reintegrate runaway and street-connected children with their families wherever safe
  • Strengthen child protection systems through communities, civil society, and state actors
  • Document best practices and share learning on child rehabilitation and reintegration

Our Founder

The person who began SATHI's journey

"Every child, no matter how far they have wandered, deserves to be found, heard, and brought safely home."

SATHI was founded in 1992 by Late Pramod Kulkarni, who was deeply moved by the lives of children he encountered at Mumbai's Victoria Terminus. Many of these children were living on railway platforms, exposed to addiction, exploitation, neglect, and the loss of family connection.

He formally registered SATHI in 1997 and dedicated his life to building an organisation focused on rescue, restoration, rehabilitation, and family reintegration. Under his leadership, SATHI grew into a national child protection organisation known for its field-based work, strong systems, and belief that every child deserves safety, dignity, and a protective family environment wherever possible.

Pramod Kulkarni is no longer with us, but his vision continues through every child SATHI helps bring back to safety.

Our Journey

How We Got Here

1992
SATHI begins in Raichur

SATHI's journey began as a project under PRERANA, working with runaway children at Raichur Railway Station.

1994
First Family Reunification

First successful family reunification of a railway child, establishing SATHI's model of home placement and restoration.

1997
Formally Registered

SATHI formally registered as a Society under the Karnataka Societies Registration Act.

2006–2010
National Expansion

Expanded through partnerships with 30+ NGOs, covering railway stations in 10 states across India.

2011–2013
ICPS Recognition

Received Open Shelter support under ICPS and contributed to national consultations on child protection in railway contexts.

2015
National Award

Received the National Award for Child Welfare from the Hon'ble President of India.

2016–17
Record Year

Rescued 10,500+ children in a single year, the highest in SATHI's history.

2018+
Street Children Work

Expanded work with street-connected children in multiple cities.

2020–2023
COVID Response

Supported vulnerable children and families affected by COVID-19, assisting over 3,600 children.

2022+
Nurture Merit Launched

Launched Nurture Merit, a national coaching initiative for talented government school students.

2023+
Migrant Programmes

Initiated programs supporting migrant workers and their children in Bengaluru, Raichur, and Yadgir districts in Karnataka.

2025
Street Children in Pune

Started working with Street-Connected Children in Pune.
