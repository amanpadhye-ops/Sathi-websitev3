People of SATHI

SATHI is not a building or a logo. It is the people who show up every morning, at every platform, in every difficult conversation.

Our Team

The people behind the work

Basavaraj Shali
Secretary, SATHI

Mr. Basavaraj Shali has been associated with SATHI since 1993, beginning his work in Raichur. With more than 30 years of experience, he has worked with children in difficult circumstances including runaway children at railway stations, street-connected children, trafficked girls, and children affected by addiction. He became Secretary of SATHI in 2014 and has since expanded the organisation's work across multiple locations in India.

Chandramouleshwara K M
Finance, HR & Administration

Mr. Chandramouleshwara K M holds degrees in B.Com and LL.B from the University of Mysore and an MBA from Karnataka State Open University. He brings over 36 years of experience across Accounts, Finance, Human Resources, Internal Audit, and General Administration, including international donor compliance. Associated with SATHI for the past 10 years, he has strengthened financial systems, grant management, statutory compliance, and FCRA filings.

Shobha Fernandes
Administrator

Ms. Shobha Fernandes holds a Bachelor of Arts with a specialisation in Office Practice and Management from St. Agnes College, Mangalore University. She brings 25 years of experience in administration and has been with SATHI for 14 years. As Administrator, she supports administrative systems, coordination processes, documentation, and day-to-day operational functioning.

Rajveer Singh (Coordinator)
Children in Contact with Railways Programme

Rajveer works SATHI's railway platforms every day, walking the same concourses, watching for the child who doesn't quite belong. He has been part of the outreach team in Pune for several years, and his patience and intuition have helped bring home hundreds of children who might otherwise have slipped away.

Govind Singh (Outreach Worker)
Children in Contact with Railways & Shelter Programme

Govind has seen every kind of story on the platform. He works across SATHI's railway outreach and shelter teams in Pune, and is known for staying with the most difficult cases — children who are frightened, non-communicative, or too young to articulate where they came from. He does not give up.

Lata Dilip Orpe (Counsellor)
Shelter & Restoration Team

Lata works in SATHI's shelter and counselling team, doing the slow, careful work of building enough trust that a child will finally say where they're from. Her cases often involve children who have experienced trauma or loss — situations that demand not just professional skill but sustained human presence.

Saroj Kumar Sah (Family Tracing Specialist)
Restoration Team

Saroj works on SATHI's most complex family tracing cases — the ones where the child has no phone number, no address, no way of pointing to a map. Over years of practice, he has developed an acute ability to read small clues: a dialect, a trade, a detail that doesn't fit. His restorations have sometimes taken years.
