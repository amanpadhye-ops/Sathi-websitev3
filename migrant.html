Children of
Migrant Families

Launched August 2025 in Raichur, Yadgir and Bangalore. Supported by Terre des Hommes Germany.

The Project

Securing the Rights of Children and Youth of Migrant Communities

Launched in August 2025, this project operates in Raichur and Yadgir, two highly underserved districts with many migrant labour families. Bangalore, a major destination for migrant workers from these districts, is also part of the project.

The project focuses on protecting the rights of children and youth from migrant communities by supporting education continuity, care and protection, access to entitlements, youth development, and stronger engagement with local systems.

Terre des Hommes Germany supports the project.

Our Approach

The project follows a community-based approach to identify vulnerable children and youth, understand their needs, and connect them with education, care, protection, and development opportunities. SATHI works with children, families, schools, hostels, government departments, and local stakeholders to reduce the risks faced by migrant families and ensure that children remain connected to learning, safety, and support systems.

Key Areas of Focus

  • Educational continuity for children of migrant families
  • Care and protection for children left without parental care
  • Children and youth participation
  • Skill training and future livelihood pathways for youth
  • Learning, nutrition, and recreation support
  • Access to government schemes and social protection
  • Sensitisation of officials and local stakeholders

What We Do

  • Re-enrol children who have dropped out of school
  • Regularise attendance for children with irregular schooling
  • Link children left behind without parental care to government hostels
  • Organise children and youth into groups to help them participate and express their concerns
  • Connect young people with skill training and referral services
  • Establish Learning and Resource Centres for education, nutrition, and recreation support
  • Guide families to access government schemes and entitlements
  • Sensitise officials to the challenges faced by children and families from migrant communities
  • Develop Kitchen Gardens in schools and communities with SHG groups
  • Awareness sessions with stakeholders and community members

Stories from the Field

Education Amid Migration
Nagaraj
18 years · Yadgir to Bengaluru · I PUC, SLN College

Nagaraj's family originally belonged to Kurukunda village in Yadgir district and migrated to Bengaluru in search of better livelihood. After his father's passing, the responsibility fell on his mother, a daily wage labourer, and two elder brothers who work as drivers.

Through the SATHI–TDH project, Nagaraj receives educational support including assistance with materials, college-related expenses, and guidance in accessing government welfare schemes. With continued support, he will build a more secure future for himself and his family.

Determination to Complete Her Degree
Yallamma
20 years · Raichur · B.A. II Year

Yallamma is from Bolamandoddi village in Raichur, currently pursuing her B.A. II Year. Her parents are elderly, and the responsibility of supporting the family largely rests on her elder brother — making her higher education increasingly difficult to sustain.

Through the SATHI–TDH project, Yallamma receives support covering college fees, study materials for competitive examinations, and other essential academic expenses. This enables her to continue her higher education without interruption.
